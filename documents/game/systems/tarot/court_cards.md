# Court Cards
This document defines the rules for Court cards (Page, Knight, Queen, King) and their interaction with Figureheads.

---

## 1. Court Cards Overview
Court cards are those with Rank Page, Knight, Queen, or King. They have special properties:
- Their effect magnitude is multiplied by **×10** (i.e., their Rank is treated as 10 for the Minor Arcana effect, but the Figurehead summoning uses the actual Rank).
- They summon a Figurehead (Upright) or Tyrant (Reversed) upon resolution.

---

## 2. Figurehead Effects

| Source Card | Role      | Figurehead Passive Effect                                   |
|-------------|-----------|-------------------------------------------------------------|
| Page        | Prince    | +1 Card Energy per Turn.                                    |
| Page        | Princess  | +1 Card Energy per Turn.                                    |
| Knight      | Champion  | +1 card drawn per Turn.                                     |
| Knight      | Dame      | +1 card drawn per Turn.                                     |
| Queen       | Matriarch | –1 cost for all Cards while alive.                          |
| King        | Patriarch | Significantly reduces the chance of drawing Reversed cards. |

For Reversed Court cards, these effects are inverted (the exact inversion is determined by the game system).

The Role assigned upon summoning a Page or Knight is determined randomly by the game system (Leaders Document, §1.1.1).

---

## 3. Role Conflicts
Only one leader of any given Role may exist at a time (Leaders Document, §1.1.2). If a Court card is played and all Roles assignable by that card are already occupied, the Court card does **not** summon a Figurehead. Instead, it resolves as a **Ten** of the same Suit and Position, with its multiplied Minor Arcana effect still applied (×10), but no Figurehead is created. The card's Count for cost purposes remains that of the original Court rank.

This rule applies to:
- Court cards played normally.
- Duplicate Court cards created by The Fool (see The Fool document, §4).

---

## 4. Figurehead Removal and Crisis of Succession
When a Figurehead is removed, the Crisis of Succession rules in the Leaders Document (§1.2) apply.
