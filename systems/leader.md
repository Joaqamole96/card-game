# Leaders: Figureheads, Tyrants, and Lineage

This document is part of the Game Rules. It defines the rules for Figureheads, Tyrants, and the lineage (court evolution) system.

---

## ARTICLE 7 – FIGUREHEADS, TYRANTS, AND LINEAGE

### §7.1 – Figureheads and Tyrants

1. **Upright Court** cards summon a Figurehead.
2. **Reversed Court** cards summon a Tyrant.
3. A Tyrant is a Figurehead with an inverted passive effect (the effect described for the Figurehead is reversed in nature).
4. Multiple leaders (Figureheads and Tyrants) may coexist, and their effects stack unless otherwise specified.

### §7.2 – Crisis of Succession

1. When the **last** leader (whether Figurehead or Tyrant) under a Player’s control is removed, a Crisis of Succession occurs.
2. The Player receives a global card effectiveness debuff according to the following table, based on the title of the removed leader:

| Title   | Debuff Magnitude |
|---------|------------------|
| Page    | –1               |
| Knight  | –2               |
| Queen   | –3               |
| King    | –4               |

3. The debuff is applied **once** regardless of how many leaders have died, and only when the last leader is removed.
4. The debuff persists until **any** new leader (Figurehead or Tyrant) is summoned via any Court card, regardless of Position.
5. Summoning a new leader immediately removes the debuff.

### §7.3 – Smiting a Tyrant

1. A Player may perform the “Smite a Tyrant” action (cost: one (1) Tick).
2. If, at the time of smiting, the Culture Domain is eighty (80) or higher, the population recognizes the tyranny and the Crisis of Succession debuff is **negated** (i.e., the debuff does not apply even though the last leader is removed).
3. Future rebellion mechanics may build upon this negation.

### §7.4 – Leader Vulnerability

Events may kill Figureheads. Any event text that indicates “may affect Figureheads” does not specify odds; the outcome is determined by the game system.

### §7.5 – Lineage (Court Evolution)

#### 7.5.1 – Heir Creation

1. When a King and a Queen have coexisted under the same Player’s control for thirty (30) consecutive Turns, they produce an Heir at the end of that Turn.
2. An Heir is not a Figurehead and provides no passive bonuses.
3. After ten (10) Turns as an Heir, it evolves into a Page (becoming a Figurehead with the Page’s +1 Card Energy bonus).
4. Only one Heir may exist at a time. If a second Heir would be produced while an Heir exists, production is delayed until the existing Heir evolves or is removed.

#### 7.5.2 – Evolution Paths

At the end of each Turn, the game system checks for evolution according to the following probabilities (chances are approximate, determined by the system):

| From       | To     | Chance   |
|------------|--------|----------|
| Page       | Knight | High     |
| Page       | Queen  | Medium   |
| Page       | King   | Low      |
| Knight     | King   | Very Low |

#### 7.5.3 – Evolution Restrictions

1. Only one leader may hold a given title at a time.
2. If a Page would evolve into a title already occupied, the evolution fails and the Page remains a Page.
3. A Knight cannot evolve into a King if a King already exists.
4. Queens and Kings cannot evolve further.

#### 7.5.4 – Internal Priority

If multiple leaders would evolve into the same title in the same Turn, the game system resolves the conflict internally (e.g., by age or identifier). The Player does not manage this process.
