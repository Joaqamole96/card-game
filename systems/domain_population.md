# Domains and Population

This document is part of the Game Rules. It defines the Domain scores and Population mechanics.

---

## ARTICLE 2 – DOMAINS

### §2.1 – Domain Types and Values

There are four Domains. Each Domain has a numerical value ranging from zero (0) to one hundred (100), inclusive. At the start of the game, all Domains are set to fifty (50).

| Domain    | Suit     | Description |
|-----------|----------|-------------|
| Military  | Swords   | Martial strength, defense, offensive capability. |
| Religion  | Wands    | Devotion, faith generation, divine favor. |
| Culture   | Cups     | Social cohesion, morale, artistic achievement. |
| Economy   | Pentacles| Wealth, trade, infrastructure. |

### §2.2 – Domain Recovery

1. Each Domain whose value is below its baseline shall increase by one (1) per Turn.
2. The baseline for each Domain is fifty (50), unless modified by a card, building, Figurehead, or event.
3. Recovery may be accelerated by buildings, Figureheads, or cards, as specified by their respective effects.

### §2.3 – Domain Thresholds

Events and card effects may require a Domain to be above or below a specified value. Such conditions shall be evaluated at the moment the effect would trigger.

## ARTICLE 3 – POPULATION

### §3.1 – Population Categories

Total Population is a concrete integer, divided into three mutually exclusive categories:

| Category   | Role                                      | Domain Contributions |
|------------|-------------------------------------------|----------------------|
| Civilian   | Farmers, Workers, Vendors, Artists        | Economy, Culture     |
| Military   | Soldiers, Guards                          | Military             |
| Elite      | Merchants, Clergy, Priests                | Economy, Religion    |

### §3.2 – Contribution to Domains

Each category contributes to Domain scores based on its size, modified by buildings, events, and card effects.

### §3.3 – Population Growth

1. Civilian population increases each Turn at a rate determined by the Economy and Culture Domains: higher values produce faster growth.
2. Military and Elite categories are recruited from the Civilian pool. Recruitment requires expenditure of resources and may be card‑driven.

### §3.4 – Population Hemorrhaging

1. If any Domain score is zero (0) or negative, Civilian population may emigrate.
2. The base emigration rate is one percent (1%) of Civilian population per Turn.
3. High Culture or Religion values reduce this loss; the reduction is determined by the game system based on the current Domain values.
